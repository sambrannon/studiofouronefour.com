Freeway Loft

The modern day loft is the vehicle for living in a manner that is not considered to be a mainstream. The loft usually exists in places that were abandoned, discarded, or thought of as uninhabitable space such as: industrial buildings, the space in between existing buildings or infrastructure, or in this case — underneath a freeway.

The “Freeway Loft” is an example of a sited and siteless design that can be manufactured all around the world and yet the area that it inhabits is such a niche that there would need to be little to no modification to the overall design whether it was to be constructed in Tokyo, Chicago, or Warsaw.

The FDY-V U3 Duet proSky and FGH-V P2 Gallery Windows by FAKRO are the perfect complement for this style of living because they are so versatile and can adapt to many locations and orientations.

The fact that the windows can be seen by millions of commuters annually would dramatically increase knowledge of and use of FAKRO’s products worldwide.
